Mavericks Mini Projects

Forks, custom builds, and other small projects designed for OS X 10.9 Mavericks.

According to a random sample of people who think OS X Mavericks is the best operating system in the universe, OS X Mavericks is the best operating system in the universe. Sadly, many software developers are unaware of this cutting-edge research, and their software does not work on Mavericks.

Below is a set of projects designed to run on OS X 10.9 Mavericks, from custom builds of existing open source software, to original creations which did not deserve their own page. Although tested primarily on Mavericks, some projects may also work on other versions of OS X from around the same era.

Audio & Video

QuickTime 10.2 for Mavericks

Mavericks ships with QuickTime Player 10.3, which is incompatible with third-party codecs. I've modified the Mountain Lion version of QuickTime to work on Mavericks.

Download

FFusion

Allows QuickTime 10.2 to play a variety of non-Apple video codecs, including 4K HEVC and VP9 if your CPU is powerful enough.

Based on RJVB's version of FFusion, which was based on the Perian Project's version of FFusion, which was based on the original by Jérôme Cornen. Also incorporates code by MaddTheSane.

Download

QuickTime Helper

Helps QuickTime open container formats such as .mkv which it can't handle natively, by remuxing into temporary formats. Best used alongside the above two projects.

App icon by Princess Cake.

Download

Preview Helper

Helps Preview open HEIC, WebP, and BPG images, by silently creating temporary copies.

Includes & relies on QuickLook Generators by maxpozdeev, dethi, and emin.

Download

Media Subscriptions Preference Pane

Your favorite podcast episodes and Youtube videos, automatically delivered to your Mac's Music and Movies folder every day. No app required, just add feeds and channels in System Preferences.

Download

Gaming

OpenEmu Lite

A Mac-native emulator for NES, SNES, NDS and all flavors of Gameboy games. Based on OpenEmu, but with a stripped-down interface and no library functionality, so you can open a game in Finder and get started right away.

Download
